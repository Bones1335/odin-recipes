Basic Curry Recipe

Description

This curry is a very simple recipe that can be made in a large pan for about 3-4 people to enjoy. That is, unless you're cooking for yourself, in which case leftovers for quite a few meals!

The base of the curry is sautéed onions in olive oil with the spices added just after browning. Then, add in your other desired ingredients, letting them mix with the spices. And finally, top it all off with some cocnut cream and water to create that delicious curry sauce. Plate it all up with a bed of rice, and enjoy a wonderful party in your mouth!

Ingredients

  • 1 large onion - diced
  • 1 table spoon olive oil
  • 2-3 table spoons curry powder of choice
  • 1/2 tablespoon garam masala
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 800gr can of (drained) chick peas
  • 1 chorizo sausage - chopped (optional)
  • 1-2 bell peppers (or other vegetable(s) of choice) - chopped
  • 400ml coconut cream*
  • 200ml water*
  1. Heat olive oil over medium heat and sauté the diced onion until browned.
  2. Add all spices and mix around to lightly toast.
  3. Incorporate your choice veggies and chick peas making sure to mix them well with the spice coating.**
  4. Add the chorizo (if desired; otherwise, skip this step).
  5. Add the coconut cream and water and mix well to incorporate the liquids all throughout the dish

* = liquid ratio dependant on pan size and how runny you want your curry to be. It should (mostly) cover your ingredients for optimal cooking, especially if you want to add a raw meat of your choice.

** = if raw meat is a desired addition, add it just before the veggies for propper coating and to add a quick cooking of the exterior for texture.
